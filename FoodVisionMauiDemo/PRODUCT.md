# Product

## Register

product

## Users

NutriLens KG is used by mobile users who want a quick, understandable check of what they are eating. The user context is casual and repeated: scan a meal, confirm the food, understand likely dietary risks, save it, then review trends later.

## Product Purpose

NutriLens KG helps users move through Scan -> Recognise -> Confirm -> Explain -> Save -> Analyse -> Recommend -> Find Nearby. It combines on-device food recognition, a local food knowledge graph, local meal history, risk trend summaries, and live nearby recommendations. Success means the user can trust the scan flow, understand the nutrition explanation, and quickly decide what to log or try next.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, calm, helpful.

The app should feel closer to Apple Health's data clarity, Lifesum's friendly nutrition tone, and Material 3's tactile mobile surfaces. It should feel like a polished health utility, not a school demo or a marketing landing page.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic MAUI template styling, repeated white cards, a single green-only palette, oversized decorative hero sections, fake marketing copy, dense programmer error messages, and visual states that rely only on color.

## Design Principles

- Lead with the user's next task: scan, confirm, save, inspect, or find nearby.
- Make risk understandable with text, shape, score, and color together.
- Keep data local and trustworthy: surface on-device inference and local storage clearly.
- Use richer hierarchy, not decoration: summary panels, grouped controls, chips, diary rows, and recommendation cards should each have a distinct role.
- Preserve familiar mobile affordances so the interface feels reliable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Support dark mode, large and extra-large font settings, screen-reader descriptions for key actions and images, touch targets of at least 44x44, user-facing error states, and risk indicators that do not rely on color alone.
